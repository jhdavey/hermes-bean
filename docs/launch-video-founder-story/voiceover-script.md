# Voiceover Script

Working title: **The System I Spent Fifteen Years Looking For**

Target read: approximately 115-125 seconds with natural pauses.

## Final Script

For more than fifteen years, I searched for the right productivity system.

Not the most complicated one.
Not the trendiest one.
Just the one that actually fit the way I work.

I tried daily planners.
Weekly layouts.
Priority lists.
Tiny notebooks.
Big notebooks.
Calendar-heavy systems.
Simple to-do lists.
And more apps than I can remember.

Some of them helped for a while.
But none of them really stuck.

At one point, I even started building my own planner layouts in Excel.
I would design the week, print the pages, fill them out, and try to keep the whole thing moving.

For a while, that was the closest I had ever come.

But eventually, even my custom system became another system I had to manage.

So I went rogue.

No planner.
No perfect layout.
Just a basic to-do list and my calendar.

But the same problems came back.

Tasks lived in one place.
Events lived somewhere else.
Reminders were scattered.
Priorities changed throughout the day.
And I was still carrying the mental load of keeping it all together.

Then, while building agentic AI systems, I realized something.

Maybe the problem wasn't that I hadn't found the perfect productivity app.

Maybe the app needed to work differently.

What if your productivity system didn't just store your tasks and events?

What if it could help you understand your day?
See what matters.
Adjust when plans change.
And act more like a personal assistant than another dashboard to maintain.

That idea became Heybean.

Heybean brings your calendar, tasks, reminders, and an AI assistant into one personal productivity system.

It's built for people who live by their calendar and to-do lists, but still feel like they're doing too much of the organizing themselves.

I built Heybean because I needed it.

Because after fifteen years of trying planners, apps, lists, templates, and routines, I wanted a system that could finally work with me.

And if you've been searching for that too,

I built Heybean for you.

## Recording Notes

- Record in a quiet room with soft surfaces nearby.
- Use your real voice, not a polished announcer voice.
- Leave a short pause after each paragraph.
- The lines "So I went rogue.", "That idea became Heybean.", and "I built Heybean for you." should have extra space around them.
- If you stumble, pause and restart the sentence. Do not stop the full recording.
