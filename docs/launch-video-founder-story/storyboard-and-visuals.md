# Storyboard And Visual Plan

## Creative Direction

Warm, thoughtful, tactile, and premium. The first half should feel like the private history of someone trying to make work and life manageable. The second half should feel like clarity arriving: Heybean is revealed as a calmer, more capable system.

Avoid robotic AI visuals, generic business stock footage, fake office people, neon circuit-board cliches, and busy feature-demo clutter.

## Rhythm

Pattern: **quiet hook -> quick memory montage -> slow frustration hold -> realization lift -> product reveal -> emotional CTA**

## Scene Plan

### 1. The Search

Approx. 0:00-0:08

Voiceover:
"For more than fifteen years, I searched for the right productivity system..."

Visual:
Close, warm desk scene. A blank notebook, pen, coffee, and a calendar edge. Slow push-in. Minimal on-screen text: "For fifteen years..."

Primary visual source:
Generated still or real desk/planner photo.

### 2. Not The Trendiest One

Approx. 0:08-0:14

Voiceover:
"Not the most complicated one. Not the trendiest one. Just the one that actually fit the way I work."

Visual:
Simple kinetic typography over tactile planner texture. Words appear as restrained stamps or pencil annotations, not loud marketing type.

Primary visual source:
Generated still plus motion typography.

### 3. Trying Everything

Approx. 0:14-0:28

Voiceover:
"I tried daily planners. Weekly layouts. Priority lists..."

Visual:
Fast but elegant montage: notebooks, planner grids, sticky notes, task app-like abstract cards, calendar blocks, handwritten lists. No readable fake text. Use quick whip/blur transitions and close-up crops.

Primary visual source:
Real personal planner materials if available, otherwise generated stills.

### 4. Almost Working

Approx. 0:28-0:36

Voiceover:
"Some of them helped for a while. But none of them really stuck."

Visual:
A neat planner page slowly gets layered with more notes, arrows, crossed-out priorities, and calendar fragments. It should feel familiar, not chaotic for its own sake.

Primary visual source:
Generated still or real planner photo with overlays added in composition.

### 5. The Excel Era

Approx. 0:36-0:47

Voiceover:
"At one point, I even started building my own planner layouts in Excel..."

Visual:
Spreadsheet-inspired weekly planner grid animates onto the screen. Printer-paper texture slides in. A stack of weekly pages grows, then pauses.

Primary visual source:
Real exported Excel planner if you have it, otherwise designed graphic recreated in HyperFrames.

### 6. Another System To Manage

Approx. 0:47-0:55

Voiceover:
"But eventually, even my custom system became another system I had to manage."

Visual:
The planner grid becomes visually heavier: more boxes, more checkmarks, more rescheduled items. The camera drifts back, making the system feel larger than the person using it.

Primary visual source:
Motion graphic built in HyperFrames.

### 7. Going Rogue

Approx. 0:55-1:03

Voiceover:
"So I went rogue. No planner. No perfect layout. Just a basic to-do list and my calendar."

Visual:
Hard simplification. Everything clears to a plain to-do list beside a calendar. Clean, sparse, almost relieving.

Primary visual source:
Designed graphic plus optional real notes.

### 8. The Same Problems

Approx. 1:03-1:15

Voiceover:
"But the same problems came back..."

Visual:
Tasks, events, reminders, and priorities separate into different zones of the frame. Lines between them break or drift apart. This should communicate fragmentation without looking frantic.

Primary visual source:
Motion graphic built in HyperFrames.

### 9. The Realization

Approx. 1:15-1:28

Voiceover:
"Then, while building agentic AI systems, I realized something..."

Visual:
Tasteful abstract system map. Nodes, calendar blocks, task cards, and assistant context align into one flow. No robot, no brain, no sci-fi glow overload.

Primary visual source:
Motion graphic built in HyperFrames, optionally supported by a generated atmospheric still.

### 10. What If It Worked Differently?

Approx. 1:28-1:41

Voiceover:
"What if your productivity system didn't just store your tasks and events? What if it could help you understand your day..."

Visual:
Fragmented task/calendar/reminder cards begin folding into a single calm workspace. The frame starts to resemble Heybean's mental model before the actual product appears.

Primary visual source:
Motion graphic built in HyperFrames.

### 11. Heybean Reveal

Approx. 1:41-1:52

Voiceover:
"That idea became Heybean."

Visual:
First clear product reveal. Use real Heybean app footage: home/day view, calendar, reminders/tasks, assistant/chat. This should be the biggest visual lift in the piece.

Primary visual source:
User-provided screen recordings plus existing screenshots.

### 12. One System

Approx. 1:52-2:02

Voiceover:
"Heybean brings your calendar, tasks, reminders, and an AI assistant into one personal productivity system."

Visual:
Polished product montage. Calendar, tasks, reminders, assistant response, and a planned day all connect. Use slow camera moves and crisp UI framing.

Primary visual source:
User-provided screen recordings.

### 13. Built For People Like This

Approx. 2:02-2:10

Voiceover:
"It's built for people who live by their calendar and to-do lists..."

Visual:
Return to human context. Desk with Heybean open. Calendar and to-do list visible. The day feels organized, not empty.

Primary visual source:
Generated still, product screenshot, or user-recorded phone/laptop shot if available.

### 14. Closing

Approx. 2:10-2:20

Voiceover:
"I built Heybean because I needed it... And if you've been searching for that too, I built Heybean for you."

Visual:
Heybean logo and clean CTA. Product screen remains visible behind or beside logo. Final line should feel personal, not salesy.

Primary visual source:
Existing logo, CTA copy, app footage.
